Intro to Muay Thai

Muay Thai

Muay Thai is a combat sport of Thailand that uses stand-up striking along with various clinching techniques.This physical and mental discipline which includes combat on shins is known as "the art of eight limbs" because it is characterized by the combined use of fists, elbows, knees, shins, being associated with a good physical preparation that makes a full-contact fighter very efficient. Muay Thai became widespread internationally in the twentieth century, when practitioners defeated notable practitioners of other martial arts.

The Modern Era

Today, Muay Thai is riding the wave of the ‘Mixed Martial Arts’ (MMA) explosion, and is the fastest growing martial art in the world. There are now thousands of muay thai academies spread across every corner of the globe. Muay Thai has also grown massively in popularity. Staying true to its roots, mauy thai continues to be effectively utilized in all MMA competition – all fighters, regardless of their speciality, require at least a working knowledge of mauy thai to stand any chance of success. The art is constantly evolving and being refined by its practitioners. New moves and techniques are being invented every day – a testament to the dynamic and ‘live’ nature of the art.
